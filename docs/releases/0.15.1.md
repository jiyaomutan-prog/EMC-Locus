# Release 0.15.1 - Signal Correction Operator UX

Version 0.15.1 is a corrective product release over the typed 0.15.0 signal
and correction contracts. It changes the operator experience and test
acceptance, not the acquisition runtime.

## Delivered

- Signaux et corrections now opens on a decision based on the measured-data
  representation: time samples or a frequency spectrum.
- Conversion temporelle presents gain, offset, lookup values, overload, and
  clipping without exposing its internal scaling-profile identity.
- Réponse fréquentielle presents amplitude and optional phase compensation,
  the usable frequency band, readable interpolation, and French CSV headings.
- Alimentation et conditionnement du capteur explains IEPE, bridge supply,
  charge conditioning, or external power without calling it DUT excitation.
- New definitions require a descriptive business name and receive an internal
  identity automatically.
- Approved references, revision history, validation, and audit views favor
  business labels. Checksums and revision identities remain available only in
  advanced diagnostics where they are useful.
- The method-library first screen uses method vocabulary and generates its
  technical identity instead of asking the author to enter it.

## Quality Corrections

- New conversions and frequency responses no longer invent a document source
  or placeholder SHA-256 evidence.
- Existing machine CSV headings remain accepted on import, while exports and
  examples use frequence_hz, amplitude_db, phase_deg, valeur_brute, and
  valeur_physique.
- Playwright runs sequentially against its one reset SQLite agent.
- A dedicated UX acceptance scenario captures and checks the main flows at
  exactly 1440 x 900 and 1280 x 720 and rejects horizontal page overflow.

## Domain Boundary

A correction attached to a reusable equipment model is not a calibration
event. A correction characterized for one serial-numbered cable, probe, or
transducer belongs to metrology and must retain its certificate, validity, and
asset identity. This release makes that boundary explicit but does not yet add
the asset-specific correction workflow.

## Limits

- No correction is applied to live samples or spectra.
- No real DAQ or instrument acquisition, FFT, station wiring, or graphical
  chain builder is added.
- No calibration certificate is converted automatically into an asset-specific
  correction.
- Automated tests demonstrate software behavior, not laboratory workflow
  fitness or EN ISO/IEC 17025 compliance.

## Validation

Release validation covers Rust formatting, lint and workspace tests; Python
compilation, unit tests and SQLite migrations; LAB CONSOLE typecheck, lint,
unit tests, production build and five sequential Playwright workflows; release
consistency, launcher smoke, browser inspection, and Git whitespace checks.
